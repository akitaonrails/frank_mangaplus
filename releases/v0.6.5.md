## FRANK MANGA+ v0.6.5

Navigation unification — every forward/back input behaves the same at chapter boundaries.

### What was broken

Up through v0.6.4 the keys and click zones each called their own navigation function:

| Input | Forward handler | Back handler |
|---|---|---|
| Space, ArrowDown, j, PageDown | `goToGroupIndex(currentGroup + 1)` | `goToGroupIndex(currentGroup - 1)` |
| ArrowLeft, ArrowRight | `pageFlip(...)` | `pageFlip(...)` |
| Left/right click zones | `pageFlip(...)` | `pageFlip(...)` |

`goToGroupIndex` and `pageFlip` both silently no-op when the target group index is out of range. So when you reached the last loaded page and pressed any forward key, nothing happened — the next chapter never got pulled. Auto-advance was supposed to kick in via the in-flight prefetch, but if that prefetch failed or hadn't fired yet, you were stuck.

Similarly, pressing back at the first page of the chapter just silently did nothing.

### What changed

Every navigation input now routes through one `advance(direction, animation)` function:

- Within the loaded scroll, it does the local move (smooth-scroll or page-flip, depending on which input triggered it).
- At the **last** loaded page going forward, it force-fetches the next chapter (12 s timeout protected, same as the prefetch) and then jumps you into it.
- At the **first** loaded page going back, it navigates to the previous chapter's URL — fresh mount, so the IntersectionObserver and scroll state stay consistent.
- Truly at the end of the title or before the first chapter, it's a clean no-op.

End result: Space, ArrowDown, ArrowLeft, the left click zone — all behave identically for forward. Same for the back inputs.
