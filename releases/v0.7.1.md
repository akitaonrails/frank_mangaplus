## FRANK MANGA+ v0.7.1

Reader nav fix at the chapter boundary.

### What was broken

When auto-advance pulled the next chapter and the user crossed the boundary, the **chapter-divider** (the dark band showing the new chapter's name) was acting as a CSS scroll-snap target — same as the page frames. With `scroll-snap-type: mandatory`, the browser was settling the viewport on the divider rather than the first page of the new chapter. End result:

- Divider pinned at the top of the viewport
- First page of the new chapter partly visible underneath but with its bottom pushed below the viewport
- Space-bar advance jumped to page **2** of the new chapter (skipping the half-visible page 1)
- Mouse-wheel scroll had to drag the half-hidden page 1 into view manually before going further

### What changed

Dropped `scroll-snap-align: start` from `.chapter-divider`. The divider now scrolls through as a transition element rather than acting as a snap point. Programmatic scrolls (from advance) land cleanly on the page frame; manual scrolls glide through the divider without pausing on it.

No other behavior change.
