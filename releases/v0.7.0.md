## FRANK MANGA+ v0.7.0

Eye-protection sepia filter for night reading.

### What's new

Reading manga on a bright LCD at night gets harsh — pure-white speech-bubble backgrounds in particular. The reader header now has a **crescent-moon toggle** (next to the page-layout icon) that cycles through four warmth levels:

| Level | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| off | unchanged, default |
| low | subtle amber tint, like a slight warm-toned monitor |
| med | noticeable sepia, like a hardware "night light" mode |
| high | heavy amber-sepia, late-night reading |

Press **`F`** to cycle through, or click the moon icon. The button shows one to three dots indicating the level. Persisted in localStorage so the choice survives reloads and chapter switches.

### How the filter works

CSS `filter: sepia(N) brightness(M) saturate(P)` applied to the whole page-stack. Sepia shifts hue toward amber without flattening the luminance range, so blacks stay black, whites become a warm cream/sepia, and the contrast of the artwork is preserved. Brightness and saturation dial in extra warmth at the higher levels.

Color manga (JoJo, etc.) keeps a recognizable palette at the low level and progressively desaturates toward the high level — that's intentional for night reading.
