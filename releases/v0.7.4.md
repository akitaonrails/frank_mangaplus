## FRANK MANGA+ v0.7.4

Hardened chapter loading. Stops the prefetch cascade and gives every failure a recovery path.

### What was broken

Several things at once when a prefetch hit a slow or failing API:

1. **Cascade**: `Promise.race` between the invoke and a 12 s timeout returned control as soon as the timeout fired, but the underlying invoke was still in flight. `fetchingNext` got reset, and the next `$effect` tick re-called `maybePrefetchNext` — which started **another** invoke for the same chapter. Both eventually completed. With multiple page moves the same thing happened for the chapter after that, and so on.
2. **No cooldown on failure**: a chapter that timed out got retried on every page move forever.
3. **No timeout on initial load**: a hung first fetch sat on the spinner indefinitely.
4. **$effect spam**: a single reactive effect handled chapter-read tracking, page-position saving, AND prefetch — so every reactive dep change (including a successful prefetch's append) immediately re-fired the prefetch check.
5. **No retry path**: failed prefetch was logged to console.warn and forgotten. The user just saw a spinner stop or never start.

### What changed

Three sets now coordinate chapter loading:

- `loadedChapterIds` — chapters whose pages are already in `loadedPages`
- `prefetchingChapterIds` — chapter IDs currently being fetched
- `failedChapterIds` — chapter IDs whose last fetch failed

A new `fetchChapter()` helper wraps `invoke('get_chapter_pages')` with the 12 s timeout and returns `null` on timeout/error rather than throwing. Both `loadInitial` and `maybePrefetchNext` go through it. `fetchingNext` is now a `$derived` of `prefetchingChapterIds.size > 0`, so multiple chapters in flight register correctly and a stale flag is impossible.

`maybePrefetchNext` checks all three sets before kicking off:
- already loaded? skip
- already in flight? skip
- failed previously (and not a forced retry)? skip

`$effect` is split. Prefetch is now in its own tiny effect that depends only on `currentPage` — page moves are the only trigger, and the same `currentPage` never fires twice.

`loadInitial` gets the same 12 s timeout and now surfaces a clear error with a **↻ Retry** button instead of an infinite spinner.

At end-of-loaded-scroll: if the next chapter is known but failed, the footer shows a labelled retry block:

> Couldn't load **#089**.
> May be rate-limited or temporarily unavailable.
> ↻ Retry #089

Clicking retry clears the failure flag and re-fetches with `force: true`.

The background prefetch indicator now names the chapter being pulled: "loading next chapter (#089)…" — so it's clear what's happening without scrolling.

### Net effect

- One inflight fetch per chapter, ever.
- Failed chapter doesn't re-fire on its own — needs a deliberate retry click.
- Initial load can't hang forever.
- The runaway "stack of chapter dividers" state from v0.7.3 isn't reachable.
