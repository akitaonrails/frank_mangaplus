## FRANK MANGA+ v0.6.4

Reader stability: layout-shift fix and auto-advance recovery.

### Pages stop bumping your scroll position while they load

The reader's `<img>` elements weren't declaring their width or height up front, so the browser couldn't reserve space until each image's bytes arrived. As pages streamed in (especially noticeable in double-page mode where two images load in parallel with possibly different aspect ratios), each arrival reflowed the page-stack and pushed your scroll position down a bit.

Fix: every `<img>` now gets the `width` and `height` HTML attributes set from the proto's dimensions. The browser reserves the correct aspect-ratio'd space before fetching, and your current visible page stays put while pages above and below stream in. CSS `max-width: 100%` / `max-height: viewport-fit` still caps the rendered size — only the *reservation* changes.

Also added explicit `overflow-anchor: auto` on the scroll container as belt-and-suspenders for any layout shift the browser doesn't already absorb.

### Auto-advance no longer gets stuck

The next-chapter prefetch fires when you're within 2 pages of the end. If that call stalled (rate-limit, network blip), `fetchingNext` stayed `true` forever and the reader would just sit there at the end of the chapter without ever pulling the next one.

Two fixes:

1. The prefetch now races against a 12 s timeout. If it hangs, the flag is released so a subsequent `currentPage` move can retry automatically.
2. A **Load next chapter ▶** button appears at the end of the loaded scroll if a known next chapter still hasn't been pulled. Click it and it forces the prefetch, even from a single position with no further scroll.

Together: auto-advance still does the right thing 99% of the time, and the 1% where it stalls now has a one-click recovery hatch instead of a dead-end.
