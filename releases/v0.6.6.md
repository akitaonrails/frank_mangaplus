## FRANK MANGA+ v0.6.6

Two reader QoL touches.

### Resume reading position per chapter

If you leave a chapter mid-read and come back to it later — either via the "Continue ▶" button on the title page, the chapter list, or any other path that loads `/reader/<chapterId>` — the reader now scrolls you back to the page you were on instead of starting from page 1.

Tracked per-chapter in localStorage. The page-position update fires whenever the visible page changes, so it's always up to date by the time you navigate away. Restoration happens immediately after the chapter's pages load, with an instant scroll so you don't see a visible animation from page 1.

If you've never visited a chapter before, or saved position is page 1, it behaves exactly like before.

### End-of-title is now obviously the end

When you reach the last chapter and try to scroll further, you used to see a small muted line of text: `— end of available chapters —`. Easy to mistake for a loading stall.

Now it's a centered card with:

> 🏁
>
> **You've reached the end**
>
> No further chapters are available right now. New releases drop on the schedule MANGA Plus publishes — check back later.
>
> `[ Back to title page ]`

Clear that the reader didn't stall, plus a one-click way back to the title page to pick something else.

### Tests
